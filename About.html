About Me

From a young age, I was fascinated by technology — my mom loves to remind me that I could turn on the computer by myself before I even started school. What began as curiosity soon grew into passion when I took Computer Science as a subject and quickly became one of the top performers in my batch. Alongside my love for tech, I’ve always been driven by teamwork and competition — I’m a state gold medalist in basketball and korfball, and proudly served as my high school’s Sports Captain.

Today, that same drive fuels my journey in San Francisco, where I’m pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science at San Francisco State University. Here, I’ve expanded my skills in AI, automation, and full-stack development, building real-world systems that blend creativity with code. My projects range from AI-powered agents that automate hiring and data workflows to personal apps like SharpenMyMind, which helps students form AI-assisted study groups.

I currently serve as an OpenAI Student Ambassador, teaching peers how to responsibly use AI for research and learning. I also founded the AI Agents Club at San Francisco State University, a growing student community exploring the future of automation and intelligent systems. Previously, I worked as an AI and Automation Intern at Dreamable, where I designed end-to-end workflows using GPT, RAG, and n8n — automating everything from document processing to recruitment pipelines.

For me, technology isn’t just about code — it’s about curiosity, creation, and connection. Whether I’m mentoring students, managing a team of developers, or experimenting with new frameworks, I’m motivated by the same question that started it all: what more can we make possible with technology?
